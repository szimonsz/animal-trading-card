The Peterbald

Though comparable to Sphynx and Oriental breeds, Peterbald is a entirely separate breed of the domestic cat (Felis Catus).

Some fun facts about Peterbalds:

  • No.1.: There are 5 different variations of Peterbald by their coat: sometimes they are completely bald!
  • No.2.: ..and the coat of this breed can change in the first two years in their lifespan! They can grow hair or even go bald - you never know!
  • No.3.: Bald Peterbalds require weekly baths and even more food - due to the lack of fur their metabolism is higher.
  • No.4.: The breed is highly affectionate, active and intelligent: they can easily be trained to walk on leash, to play fetch or even to use the toilet!
  • No.5.: ... This list could go on forever as these lovely feline companions are full with life and suprise!

Being an Oriental-type of cat breed, the Peterbald is long and slim. Her head is shaped like an isosceles triangle with large projecting ears and almond-shaped eyes. She has long and elegant neckline and her body is muscular. Her legs are thin and slender and her tail resembles a long whip.
